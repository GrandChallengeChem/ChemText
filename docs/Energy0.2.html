0.2 Energy and Chemistry

Chemical reactions. Energy flow.

Every chemical reaction is accompanied by a flow of energy. Chemistry is energy in action.

A chemical reaction involves the transformation of one group of matter (we call them reactants) into another group of matter (which we call products). Such a transformation is always accompanied by a transfer of energy. In some cases, energy is released. In other cases, energy is absorbed.

Depending upon how we manage the transformation (the reaction) that energy may be transferred to us in the form of work or in the form of heat (or a combination of the two). We can burn gas in a barrel or we can use it to drive a car – but it is the same reaction.

Natural gas consists mostly of an organic molecule called methane. Its chemical formula is CH4. The other principal component is ethane, whose formula is C2H6. In April 2018, the analysis of natural gas at the Victoria Square Station which supplies natural gas to the Greater Toronto area in Canada, was 93.93% methane and 4.27% ethane. The composition is measured regularly and the monthly averages posted. Different compositions provide different heating values. We will explore this in more depth later.
