Chapter 0.1 - Introduction

Leaving Planet Earth. The Age of Exploration Begins Anew.

Chemistry provides the motivation and the power to move out there. It will also provide the tools to allow us to thrive.

Much of recorded history has involved the expansion of humans into new territories. Such expansion has been critical for the growth of civilization. It must also be recognized that it has often had disasterous implications for some of those involved such as for indigenous peoples. But the Colonial Era started to wind-down a century ago and has been slowly replaced by a global village. Fifty years ago, the space race between Russia and the United States fueled our first extra-planetary adventures, culminating with the series of lunar explorations as part of NASA's Apollo Space Program. Since then, our attention has been more on near-earth orbit with space station development. It seems, however, that the world is poised to try again, this time heading to Mars with the ultimate goal of establishing permanent settlements under these advserse conditions. What will it take to survive on the moon? on Mars? We can be certain that chemistry will play a central role.

Food and resources will be essential for the longterm survival of any new outposts. This was true even when our ancestors landed on comparatively fertile locations, such as North America. Those first winters were treacherous. Establishing a lunar or martian outpost will be even more demanding. The change in gravity will change the way plants grow and the way water percolates through the soil. The chemical makeup of the soil itself will need to adjusted with fertilizers to ensure the plants can grow there. Protection from radiation will be essential to ensure both human and plant survival. Mineral resources will be important to develop. Can we find and extract inorganic compounds essential for human and plant health (thinking here of phosphates or nitrates). What about metals for construction: iron, nickel, chromium, and so forth? Will these be scientific outposts or will there be economic motivations to enable permanent residents? Chemistry plays other important roles in space exploration and colonization. Chemical propellants are essential for launch vehicles. Power sources based on fuel cells will be essential due to their efficiency.

Chemistry's role is fundamental throughout space-based missions to other worlds; from getting there, to establishing a base, to securing a colony. Chemistry will be important. Here we want to explore chemistry by studying its role in space colonization, making this subject the context in which we will learn about chemistry, its theories and principles, its approach to problem solving and critical thinking.
